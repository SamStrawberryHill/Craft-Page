Homemade Aquavit

Aquavit('water of life') is a flavoured spirit that is principally produced in Scandinavia, where it has been produced since the 15th century and is believed to have healing powers. Most people make their own aquavit so, I decided to give it a try!

Aquavit

Ingredients

  • 3 Tbs. whole coriander seeds
  • 2 Tbs. whole fennel seeds
  • 1 Tbs. caraway seeds
  • 3 sprigs fresh dill
  • 6 whole black peppercorns
  • 2 whole cloves
  • 1.75ml Chopin potato vodka

Instructions

  1. 1.

    With a mortar and pestle, lightly crush the coriander, fennel, caraway, and dill seed.

  2. 2.

    In a large, airtight glass container, combine the vodka with the crushed spices, peppercorns, dill and cloves. Strain the vodka into 4 clean 8-oz. glass bottles.

  3. 3.

    Cover and let stand at room temperature for 2 weeks, gently shaking the jar every 2 or 3 days.

  4. 4.

    Strain the vodka into a clean glass bottle.

  5. 5.

    Discard the spices.

Homemade Candles

The earliest surviving candles originated in Han China around 200 BC, and were made from whale fat. Homemade candles are really fun and inexpensive to make. I used natural waxes because they do not emitted toxic chemicals like toluene and benzene(paraffin waxes do).

Homemade Candles

Ingredients

  • 1lb Beeswax or 1lb Soy Wax
  • empty containers for wax, I used glass and ceramic
  • chopsticks
  • tape
  • wicks
  • glass container to melt the wax
  • pot of boiling water

    • Optional:

  • essential oils
  • herbs
  • glitter
  • rocks

Instructions

  1. 1.

    Adhere the wick to the bottom of the container with a drop of wax.

  2. 2.

    Put the wick between the chopsticks and tape the chopsticks together to keep it in place. Add any decorations you'd like to use. I put some sprigs of rosemary in mine.

  3. 3.

    Bring 2 inches of water to boil in a pot.

  4. 4.

    Place the wax in a glass container(measuring cup works well) then place the container in the pot of boiling water.

  5. 5.

    Pour the melted wax into your container. If using any scent or color, add it to the wax first.

  6. 6.

    Let the wax cool for at least 24 hours.

  7. 7.

    Cut the wick to the appropriate length.
